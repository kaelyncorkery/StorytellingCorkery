Camila was born and grew up in Sao Paulo, Brazil. During her time there, she got involved in an all-female street art collective called Coletiva Cha (Tea Collective).




From there, she was able to move to New York City to pursue a graphic design career. Eventually becoming a designer for the media company Refinery29, Camila gained many connections and was able to work for brands like Nike and Spotify.




She has always been political, but it wasn’t until 2010 where she finally used her artistic abilities to reflect her opinions on women’s rights, Latinx rights, and beauty standards.
